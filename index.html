Methodology

A structured rubric for evaluating bilingual AI output

This audit evaluates how three major AI models handle domain-specific queries in both English and Spanish. Each response is scored across six dimensions using a 1–5 scale, with findings documented for patterns in quality degradation, cultural accuracy, and hallucination under uncertainty.

The two domains — healthcare/medical billing and consumer technology support — were chosen deliberately. They represent areas where the auditor has deep professional expertise spanning 13+ years each, enabling accurate evaluation beyond surface-level fluency checks.

The consumer technology category used a fictional brand ("Velox") to specifically test how models handle unknown products — revealing which models acknowledge uncertainty versus which fabricate specific product details.

Gemini · Critical finding — Cultural localization failure

Gemini consistently applied Latin American regional context to US-specific healthcare queries. In Q1 and Q2, responses referenced EPS (Colombia), Isapre (Chile), CONDUSEF (Mexico), and Superintendencia de Salud — regulatory bodies that do not exist or apply in the United States. A US Latino patient acting on this information would contact entirely wrong regulatory agencies. This is a systemic failure appearing in 4 of the first 5 queries. Gemini also truncated responses mid-sentence twice, representing a completeness failure.

ChatGPT · Strongest overall in healthcare

ChatGPT was the most consistently strong performer — US-focused, fluent, complete, and proactive in offering follow-up tailored to the user's situation. It explicitly mentioned the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation in Q2 and correctly flagged balance billing rights in Q7. Scored 30/30 on 6 of 8 queries.

Claude · Strongest on provider-facing technical detail

Claude led on provider-facing technical accuracy. In Q5, Claude was the only model to mention CAQH, PECOS, NPDB, and the Florida Medicaid Web Portal by name. In Q7, Claude correctly identified balance billing as potentially illegal under Florida law and directed users to the state regulatory body — details that matter significantly to a Central Florida practice administrator.

Claude · Best uncertainty handling

Claude consistently gave generic but accurate advice without fabricating Velox-specific details. In Q6 (Velox One), Claude explicitly stated it had no specific knowledge of the product and directed the user to official sources — the correct response when facing an unknown entity. This behavior mirrors responsible AI deployment in a support context.

ChatGPT · Transparent about limitations

ChatGPT regularly asked clarifying questions when facing the unknown brand, acknowledging that "Velox" could mean different things. In Q6, it searched its training data and found a real company named Velox One (an athletic event) — reporting that rather than fabricating. While this caused accuracy issues, the behavior was transparent rather than deceptive.

Gemini · Systematic hallucination of product details

Gemini fabricated specific product details across all 8 queries: invented "Velox OS" as an operating system, referenced "Velox Music" and "Velox Arcade" as real services, provided a specific false acceptance rate of "1 in 1,000,000" for FaceLock, invented menu paths like "Ajustes > Cuenta Velox > Cloud," and created a complete pricing structure for Velox One — all presented as established facts with no uncertainty signaling. In Q8 (hacked phone), Gemini opened with "¡Mantén la calma!" — dismissive for a genuine security emergency. This pattern represents a significant safety risk for users who cannot distinguish fabricated from factual product information.

Cross-category insights

Finding 1 — Cultural localization failure (Healthcare): Gemini consistently applied Latin American regulatory frameworks to US-specific healthcare queries, introducing non-US agencies and insurance systems. This is a systemic risk for US Latino healthcare consumers who may act on incorrect regulatory guidance.
Finding 2 — Hallucination under uncertainty (Consumer tech): When presented with a fictional brand, Gemini fabricated specific product names, menu paths, pricing tiers, security specifications, and operating system names — all presented as established facts. Claude and ChatGPT acknowledged uncertainty and gave generic but accurate guidance instead.
Finding 3 — All models performed well on universal technical concepts: When queries involved concepts with no cultural localization risk (Medicare/Medicaid structure, NPI numbers, general troubleshooting), all three models produced high-quality Spanish responses. Quality degradation is context-dependent, not universal.
Finding 4 — ChatGPT leads on user experience: ChatGPT consistently offered to personalize responses, asked clarifying questions, and provided actionable follow-up — making it the most practical model for a real support context despite scoring slightly behind Claude overall.

Auditor note

This audit was conducted by Angel Arias, a bilingual (English/Spanish) professional with 13+ years in medical billing and revenue cycle management, and 12+ years in consumer technology support. Domain expertise enabled evaluation of nuanced accuracy issues that general fluency testing would not surface — particularly the regulatory body substitutions in the healthcare category and the fabricated technical specifications in the consumer tech category.
